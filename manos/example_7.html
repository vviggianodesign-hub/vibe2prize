From Prototype to Requirements — Reversing the Traditional Flow

07

Functional Extraction

User Flows

Map clickable paths to formal step-by-step requirements. Every screen transition in the prototype must have a corresponding functional specification.

Business Rules

Identify logic embedded in the prototype (e.g., validation triggers, conditional visibility) and document as immutable business rules.

Traceability

Maintain a direct link between derived requirements and prototype screens to ensure no validated behavior is lost during hardening.

Non-Functional Gaps

Performance & Scale

Define response times, concurrent user limits, and data volume thresholds that the prototype stubs did not address.

Security Standards

Specify authentication methods, authorization levels, and data encryption requirements for production environments.

Observability

Document logging, monitoring, and alerting requirements essential for enterprise operational stability.

Assumptions Register

Technical Constraints

Surface all assumptions made regarding browser support, network latency, and integration availability during prototyping.

Data Integrity

Validate assumptions about data quality, source system reliability, and transformation logic with stakeholders.

Client Sign-Off

Formalize the requirements baseline. A signed document is the single most important risk mitigation in the delivery process.

KEY PRINCIPLE: Requirements grounded in a validated prototype are more accurate than those derived from abstract interviews.
